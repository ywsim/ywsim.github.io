Human-Machine Interface: Whole-body Haptic Feedback + Mocap

Youngwoo Sim,
RoboDesignLab, UIUC

Preliminary Demos

HMI serves as a physical channel between bipedal robots and a humanoid pilot. It can capture human center of pressure, center of mass, torso position & orientation, ground reaction force, ankle and wirst positions. But more importantly, it can synchronize the pilot with the robot by providing haptic forces to human torso to reduce the spatio-temporal mismatch in dynamics of two underactuated systems.

Our favorite demo is to have professors as pilot then apply gentle external wrenches. Since the wrenches are applied almost immediately, it almost feels like you are at an amusement park trying new things. Then we proceed to explain the core philosophy behind this entire contraption. We are investigating the synchrony of two dynamical sytsems.

  1. estabilish bilateral connection between the pilot to the humanoid
  2. we kick the robot, and proportional wrench is applied to the pilot
  3. the pilot uses reflex to come to balance
  4. that human reflex is projected back to robot, bringing the robot back to balance

Integration with robots

  • reduced order model based intermediate reference

Design Optimization

The HMI is equipped with 4x linear actuators (max 150N each) which are connect to a backplate of a wearable vest. Since the pilot's torso is in 6D, we are expecting some parasitie wrenches applied in 2-dim subspace (recall we only have 4 actuators). Design optimization was carried out to minimize parasitic wrenches using genetic algorithm. .

Implementation Details

A real-time linux computer (labview c-rio) serves as a middleware: collects and broadcasts commands and sensor feedbacks. Motor drivers and sensor integration boards were mounted on the rear face of the HMI.
