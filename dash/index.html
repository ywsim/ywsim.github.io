Human-Machine Interface: Whole-body Haptic Feedback + Mocap

Youngwoo Sim
RoboDesignLab, UIUC

Preliminary Demos

HMI serves as a physical channel between bipedal robots and a humanoid pilot. It can capture human center of pressure, center of mass, torso position & orientation, ground reaction force, ankle and wrist positions. More importantly, it can synchronize the pilot with the robot by providing haptic forces to the human torso, reducing the spatio-temporal mismatch between the dynamics of two underactuated systems.

Our favorite demo is to have professors as pilots and then apply gentle external wrenches. Because the wrenches are applied almost immediately, it feels like an amusement park experience. We then explain the core philosophy:

  1. Establish a bilateral connection between the pilot and the humanoid.
  2. We kick the robot, and a proportional wrench is applied to the pilot.
  3. The pilot reflexively comes to balance.
  4. This human reflex is projected back to the robot, bringing it to balance.

Design Optimization

The HMI is equipped with 4 linear actuators (max 150N each) connected to a wearable vest backplate. Since the pilotâ€™s torso is in 6D and only 4 actuators exist, parasitic wrenches appear in a 2D subspace. We minimized these parasitic wrenches using a genetic algorithm.

Implementation Details

A real-time Linux computer (LabVIEW cRIO) acts as middleware: collecting and broadcasting commands and sensor feedback. Motor drivers and integration boards are mounted on the rear face of the HMI.
